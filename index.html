What is Nirbhayaa IDS?

Welcome to Nirbhayaa IDS, a collaborative hub of innovators, engineers, and visionaries committed to fortifying the future of intelligent mobility. Our mission is to uncover and counteract the hidden threats that challenge autonomous systems—ranging from data spoofing and DDoS attacks to subtle behavioral anomalies. Through cutting-edge research, real-time detection mechanisms, and intelligent defense strategies, we aim to build a safer digital and physical landscape for autonomous vehicles and smart infrastructures. Whether you're a researcher, cybersecurity enthusiast, or future-forward technologist, Nirbhayaa IDS invites you to join our pursuit of resilience, trust, and transparency in the age of autonomy.

Our Domain

 The advancement of Intelligent Transport Systems (ITS) has reshaped the modern transportation landscape by integrating data processing and communication technologies to improve safety, efficiency, and sustainability. A major outcome of this evolution is the development of autonomous vehicles (AVs), which rely on real-time data, sensors, and algorithms to operate without human intervention. While AVs can provide a lot in the way of reduced traffic congestion and human error, they also come with serious safety concerns. The lack of direct human involvement makes such systems highly vulnerable to cyber threats. Sophisticated hacks can exploit system vulnerability, with the potential to disrupt vehicle operation, place lives in jeopardy, and shake public trust. As there are more AVs on the roads, assured safe integration with existing infrastructure is critical. Traditional antivirus software might not be sufficient to counter the difficulties created by new threats, thus calling for the adoption of adaptive and intelligent security systems. This paper discusses the cybersecurity concerns of autonomous cars and emphasizes the need to adopt sophisticated protection mechanisms to ensure safe and reliable transportation networks

 The development of an intrusion detection system for autonomous vehicles faces several intertwined challenges. First, state-of-the-art anomaly detection methods—such as transformer-based, semi-supervised clustering-based, and hybrid CNN-Kalman filter model-based approaches—often turn out to be too resource-intensive, rely on extensive amounts of labeled data or preestablished types of anomalies, and lack the ability to adapt to unknown driving patterns in resource-constrained embedded environments. Second, camera-spoofing defenses such as adversarial training or input pre-processing are static and do not address the continually evolving nature of attacks over the lifespan of a car; nor do they consider how detected threats are communicated to human drivers, which could cause confusion or delayed response. Third, existing deep learning-based DDoS detection in SDN scenarios tends to focus on either feature extraction or model adaptation only, without learning spatial and temporal dependencies simultaneously using attention mechanisms, offering little transparency to network operators, and cannot be executed sustainably in real time on resource-limited controllers. Finally, LiDAR spoofing research has so far covered either portions of attack types and vehicle–attacker states. It needs covering the gaps with lightweight, environment-aware models, fusion of multiple modalities, explainability and human-friendly interfaces, over-the-air update capability, and thorough validation across diverse scenarios.

What do we provide?

Anomaly Detection System



Identify anomalies that can be occured in an autonomous vehicle by analyzing the telemetry data such as Speed, Acceleration and Heading angle of the vehicle.

DDoS Attack Identification



Identify DDoS attacks in SDN-based autonomous vehicles using a CNN-BiLSTM with Transformer-based feature transformation, dynamically prioritizing key features, and using SHAP for interpretable, generalizable, robust detection.

Camera Adversarial Attack Identification



Detect and classify adversarial camera attacks on autonomous vehicles by analyzing image perturbations and evaluating physical robustness, applying deep learning–based defense mechanisms for real-time perception reliability and overall system safety.

LiDAR Spoofing Attack Detection



Develop Autoencoder based classification model to detect and classify LiDAR spoofing attacks. Enhance robustness by modeling diverse attack scenarios and incorporating physical sensor constraints.
